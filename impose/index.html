impose

Pronunciation: /ɪmˈpəʊz/
verb

Definition

To force something to be accepted or put in place.

Example Sentences

  1. The government decided to impose new taxes.
  2. He didn't want to impose his beliefs on others.
  3. They impose strict rules in the classroom.

Synonyms & Antonyms

Synonyms: enforce, inflict, dictate Antonyms: remove, lift, abolish

Etymology

From Latin 'imponere' meaning 'to place upon'.
