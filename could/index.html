could

Pronunciation: /kʊd/
modal verb

Definition

Used to indicate possibility or ability in the past.

Example Sentences

  1. She could swim when she was five.
  2. If I had more time, I could help you.
  3. He could hear the music from next door.

Synonyms & Antonyms

Synonyms: might, was able to, had the ability Antonyms: couldn't, cannot, can't

Etymology

From Old English 'cuðe', past tense of 'cunnan', meaning 'to know, be able'.
