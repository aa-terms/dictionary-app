radar

Pronunciation: /ˈreɪ.dɑːr/
noun

Definition

A system for detecting the presence, direction, distance, and speed of objects using radio waves.

Example Sentences

  1. The airplane was tracked by radar throughout its flight.
  2. Weather radar helps predict storms.
  3. The ship used radar to avoid obstacles in the fog.

Synonyms & Antonyms

Synonyms: sonar, detector, scanner Antonyms: blind spot, invisibility, obscurity

Etymology

Acronym from 'Radio Detection and Ranging', coined in 1941.
